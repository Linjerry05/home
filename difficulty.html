Difficulty is an option in Minecraft that has an impact on Gameplay, depending on your skills.

There are 4 diffculty options in Minecraft. They are Peaceful, Easy, Medium, and Hard


Peaceful

The first difficulty choice is Peaceful. In peaceful difficulty, most hostile mobs (more about mobs later) can't spawn.

The hunger bar of the player also doesn't go down, so there is no need to find food and eat.

Despite this, it is still possible to die in this difficulty from fall damage, drowning, or burning.

This mode is for new players who are playing the game for the first time, that want to get used to the game mechanics.


Easy

The second difficulty is Easy. In easy difficulty, most mobs do less damage in normal difficulty.

For example, a Vindicator's attack that does 13 damage in normal mode does only 9 damage in easy mode.

Your health bar also only goes down to ten (out of 20) once your hunger bar reaches zero.

This means that getting killed from starvation is not possible in easy difficulty.

This mode is for new players that are familiar with the game mechanics, and want to try full survival difficulty for themselves.


Normal

The third difficulty is Normal. In normal difficulty, mobs do standard damage.

Unlike easy difficulty, once your hunger bar reaches 0, your health bar goes down to one.

Like easy difficulty, getting killed by starvation is not possible.

However, since you have only 1 HP, it leaves you very vulnerable to getting killed.

This mode is for average players who have a casual playstyle.


Hard

The fourth and final difficulty is Hard. In hard difficulty, mobs do additional damage.

Using the same example from before, a Vindicator does 13 damage in normal mode.

However, in hard mode, it does 19 damage, almost enough to one instantly kill an unarmored player.

Unlike easy and normal mode, one can, in fact, die from starvation in hard mode.
